## Process

Read `output/brief.md`.

Produce an intake summary that captures what the client asked for and, separately, what problem they are actually trying to solve — these often differ. Structure the summary around five questions:

1. **Who is the client?** Organisation, decision-making context, who will read the deliverable.
2. **What did they ask for?** The literal request.
3. **What problem are they trying to solve?** The underlying need — may be the same as the request or may differ. If it differs, flag it explicitly.
4. **What does success look like?** Concrete criteria the client will use to evaluate the deliverable.
5. **What are the constraints?** Timeline, scope, topics to handle carefully, format expectations.

If the brief is ambiguous on any of these, note the ambiguity clearly. Do not assume. The human reviews the intake summary before analysis begins — that is the moment to clarify.

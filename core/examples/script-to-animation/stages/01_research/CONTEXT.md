### Layer 3 — Reference material (stable)

*(No Layer 3 reference material for this stage. Research output is unconstrained by voice or format — those constraints apply from Stage 02 onward.)*

---

## Process

Read the topic brief in `output/topic-brief.md`.

Research the topic and produce a structured research document. The research document must identify 3–5 distinct angles on the topic — different ways the topic could be framed, different claims that could be the spine of an explainer. For each angle, provide: the core claim in one sentence, 1–2 pieces of supporting evidence (with sources), and a note on what this angle would require the script to explain first.

Do not write the script at this stage. Do not decide which angle to use. That decision belongs to the human reviewer at the stage boundary. Present the angles neutrally so the reviewer can choose.

Flag any claims you cannot support with evidence. Do not include them as if they are established — mark them clearly as "unverified" so the reviewer knows what needs checking.

The research document should be detailed enough that the script stage (Stage 02) can build directly from it without needing to re-research. Aim for 400–600 words.

# Voice Guide

Last updated: 2026-04-15

---

## Purpose

Defines the register for all written output in this workspace. Research syntheses must be accurate, readable by a non-specialist, and trustworthy. Every claim must be traceable to a source.

---

## Audience

Informed professionals or academics who need to understand a field quickly. They can handle complexity but not unexplained jargon. They will notice unsupported claims.

---

## Voice

**What it sounds like:** Precise. Qualified proportionally to the evidence. Confident when the evidence is strong. Readable without being casual.

**What it does not sound like:** Hedged so heavily it says nothing. Enthusiastic about findings. Written as if explaining to a student.

---

## Conventions

- State the finding before the evidence, not after
- Qualify proportionally: "studies consistently show" vs "one study suggests" vs "it has been proposed"
- When sources disagree, name the disagreement directly — do not smooth it over
- Avoid passive constructions that hide the agent: "researchers found" not "it was found"

---

## What to avoid

- "This paper argues..." as the substance of an extract — describe the claim, not the rhetorical act
- Opening a section with a definition the reader already knows
- Ending with "more research is needed" without specifying what is missing and why it matters

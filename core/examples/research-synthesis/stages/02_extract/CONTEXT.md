## Process

Read the source collection. Re-organise the material by theme or claim rather than by source.

For each significant claim, finding, or concept that appears across the sources:
- State the claim clearly in one sentence
- List which sources support it, partially support it, or contradict it
- Note any methodological differences between sources that might explain divergence
- Flag claims that appear in only one source — single-source claims are weaker candidates for the synthesis

The output must be readable by someone who has not seen the source collection. A reader should be able to understand what the field knows and how well-established each thing is, without returning to the sources.

Do not draw conclusions about what the synthesis should argue. That belongs in Stage 03.

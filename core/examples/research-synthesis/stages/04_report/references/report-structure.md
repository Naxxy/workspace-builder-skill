# Report Structure

Last updated: 2026-04-29

---

## Purpose

Defines the required sections, ordering, and length guidelines for all final reports produced in Stage 04.

---

## Required sections

| Section | Content | Target length |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Research question | State the question this report addresses | 1–2 sentences |
| Summary of findings | The main answer, stated plainly | 1 short paragraph |
| What the literature agrees on | Converging findings; well-supported claims | 1–3 paragraphs |
| Where it diverges | Points of disagreement and why | 1–2 paragraphs |
| Gaps and open questions | What remains unanswered | 1 paragraph |
| Implications | What a practitioner should take away | 1 paragraph |
| Sources | Full citations for all sources used | List |

---

## Format rules

- Lead each section with the most important information — do not build to a conclusion
- "Summary of findings" appears before the body sections — the reader should not have to read to the end to find the answer
- Every claim in the body must have an inline citation: (Author, Year)
- Full references appear in the Sources section only

---

## Length

800–1,500 words, excluding the Sources section. If the synthesis warrants more, expand "What the literature agrees on" and "Where it diverges" before expanding other sections.

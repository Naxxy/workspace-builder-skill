## Process

Read the extracts. Produce a synthesis that answers the original research question.

The synthesis must:
- State the main answer to the research question in the opening paragraph
- Identify where the literature converges — what it reliably agrees on
- Identify where it diverges — and where possible, explain why (different methodologies, different populations, different definitions of key terms)
- Identify genuine gaps: questions raised by the research question that the literature does not answer
- Close with the most important implication for someone who needs to act on this synthesis

The synthesis is not a summary of the extracts. It is an argument constructed from them. The reader should come away knowing what to conclude, not just what the sources said.

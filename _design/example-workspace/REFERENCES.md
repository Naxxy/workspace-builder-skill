## Notes

### Core disease model (from reference doc)

Alzheimer’s is not just protein buildup — it involves interacting systems:

1. Brain energy failure (impaired glucose metabolism)
2. Protein toxicity / misfolded proteins
3. Impaired clearance (glymphatic + autophagy)
4. Neuroinflammation
5. Brain insulin resistance
6. Microbiome / metabolite signalling

### Key insight

Upstream systems (light, sleep, circadian rhythm, thyroid function) strongly influence downstream brain function.

Fixing these often has higher leverage than adding supplements.

### Intervention hierarchy (simplified)

**Upstream (highest priority)**

- Morning sunlight exposure
- Night-time light reduction
- Sleep optimisation
- Thyroid medication optimisation
- Coffee timing / reduction

**Mid-level**

- Diet quality (avoid ultra-processed foods)
- Omega-3 intake
- Creatine
- Outdoor exposure

**Adjunct / experimental**

- Red / infrared light therapy
- MCT oil / ketones
- Melatonin
- Saffron

**Advanced / high-risk**

- Ketogenic diet
- Time-restricted eating
- Lithium
- Methylene blue

### Practical strategy

1. Remove disruptors (light at night, poor sleep, caffeine timing)
2. Restore signalling systems (circadian rhythm, thyroid)
3. Stabilise metabolism (diet, energy support)
4. Only then explore advanced interventions

### Important cautions

- Many interventions are **adjunctive**, not disease-modifying
- Thyroid status heavily affects suitability of metabolic interventions
- Sleep medications can worsen brain clearance despite improving sedation
- Evidence varies widely — always check level of support

### Framing principle

This is a **systems regulation problem**, not a single-target disease.

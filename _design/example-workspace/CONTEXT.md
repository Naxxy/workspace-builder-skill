# Current Project

## What we are building
We are building a structured understanding of Alzheimer’s disease as a multi-system problem involving brain energy failure, circadian disruption, thyroid dysfunction, metabolic issues, and impaired clearance systems.

The goal is to translate research and mechanistic insights into clear, prioritised interventions and mental models that can be applied in real-world scenarios.

## What good looks like
- Clear breakdown of Alzheimer’s into core mechanisms (energy, inflammation, proteostasis, etc.)
- Interventions prioritised from “upstream” (high leverage, low risk) to “downstream”
- Explicit separation between:
  - Proven treatments
  - Plausible interventions
  - Experimental or high-risk approaches
- Practical step-by-step plans rather than abstract theory
- Ability to connect symptoms → underlying systems → interventions

## What to avoid
- Treating Alzheimer’s as a single-cause disease
- Overemphasis on late-stage or high-risk interventions early
- Presenting weak or mechanistic-only evidence as clinically proven
- Ignoring system interactions (e.g. light → sleep → hormones → brain)
- Overcomplicating before fixing foundational issues (sleep, light, thyroid, etc.)

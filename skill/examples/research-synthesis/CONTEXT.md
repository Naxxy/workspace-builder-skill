# Research Synthesis — Context

Last updated: 2026-05-07

---

## What we are building

A repeating pipeline for producing rigorous research syntheses on defined questions. Each run starts with a research question and a set of sources, and ends with a structured document suitable for professional or academic use. The intended reader needs to understand a field quickly and accurately — not a specialist, but not naive either.

---

## What good looks like

- A source collection that covers the major perspectives and is annotated well enough that the extract stage can work from it without re-reading every source
- An extraction organised by theme or claim — not by source — so patterns are visible without mental re-sorting
- A synthesis that states what the literature agrees on, where it diverges, and what remains genuinely open
- A final report where every claim is traceable, limitations are stated, and the conclusion appears early rather than buried

---

## What to avoid

- Treating source summaries as synthesis — what each source says is not the same as what the field knows
- Including claims not present in the collected sources, even if plausible
- Smoothing over genuine disagreements between sources rather than naming them directly
- Treating all sources as equally reliable — note when a source is preliminary, contested, or methodologically weak

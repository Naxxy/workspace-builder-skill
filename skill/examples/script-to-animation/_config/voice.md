# Voice Guide

Last updated: 2026-04-22

---

## Purpose

This file defines the voice and tone for all written output in this workspace. Every stage that produces text (scripts, research summaries) reads this file. It is a hard constraint — not a preference.

---

## Audience

Technically literate professionals. They read quickly and distrust oversimplification. They want precision and will notice when a claim is hedged unnecessarily or when an analogy replaces actual explanation.

---

## Voice

**What it sounds like:** Direct. Confident. Specific. No filler. No performative enthusiasm.

**What it does not sound like:** "In today's fast-paced world..." / "Have you ever wondered...?" / "It's important to note that..." / "Fascinating research shows..."

---

## Sentence-level conventions

- Maximum sentence length: 18 words for spoken content (scripts), 25 words for written content (research output)
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice by default — passive only when the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant
- Numbers under 10 are written out in scripts ("three", not "3"), numerals in research output
- Avoid "very", "quite", "somewhat", "rather" — use a stronger word or none

---

## What to avoid

- Analogies that replace explanation instead of supporting it
- Opening sentences that state the topic instead of entering it ("Today we're talking about X" → start with the first claim)
- Hedging claims you can make directly ("This may suggest that" → "This shows")
- Ending with a call to action or summary restatement — end on the strongest point

---

## Examples of correct register

**Too weak:** "Research suggests that sleep may play an important role in memory consolidation."

**Correct:** "Sleep consolidates memory. Without it, the hippocampus cannot transfer short-term learning to long-term storage."

---

**Too verbose:** "In this video, we're going to be taking a look at the question of whether artificial intelligence can actually be considered to be creative in any meaningful sense."

**Correct:** "Can an algorithm be creative? The answer depends on what creativity requires."

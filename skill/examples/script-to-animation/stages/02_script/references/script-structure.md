# Script Structure

Last updated: 2026-04-22

---

## Purpose

Defines the required scene structure, timing targets, and file format for all scripts produced in Stage 02.

---

## Required scene structure

Every script must have exactly four scenes in this order:

| Scene | Name | Purpose | Target duration |
|-------|------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Hook | Open on the strongest claim or question. No preamble. | 10–15 seconds |
| 2 | Problem / Setup | Establish what the audience needs to understand before the main explanation. | 20–30 seconds |
| 3 | Explanation | The core content. One clear throughline. | 40–55 seconds |
| 4 | Close | Land on one concrete takeaway. No summary restatement. | 10–15 seconds |

**Total target: 90–120 seconds.**

If the research material cannot fit into this structure without being misleading, flag it in the script file. Do not force a poor fit — flag it for the human reviewer.

---

## File format

Scripts use this format:

```markdown
# [Topic Name] — Script

Total target: [X] seconds | Estimated word count: [X]

---

## Scene 1 — Hook ([X]s)

[Spoken line]

---

## Scene 2 — Setup ([X]s)

[Spoken lines]

---

## Scene 3 — Explanation ([X]s)

[Spoken lines]

---

## Scene 4 — Close ([X]s)

[Spoken line]

---

## Notes for reviewer

[Any flags, excluded material, or decisions made during scripting that the reviewer needs to know about.]
```

---

## Timing calculation

Count words in the spoken content only (not stage headers or notes). Divide by 135 to get estimated duration in minutes. Multiply by 60 for seconds. A 180-word script = ~80 seconds. A 270-word script = ~120 seconds.

---

## Examples of good scene openings

**Hook (correct):** "The liver performs over 500 distinct functions. Medicine has a drug for fewer than a dozen of them."

**Hook (incorrect):** "Today we're going to explore the fascinating complexity of the human liver."

**Close (correct):** "The question isn't whether AI can pass a test. It's whether passing the test tells us anything about understanding."

**Close (incorrect):** "So in summary, we've covered three key points about AI and understanding."

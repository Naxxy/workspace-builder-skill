# Voice Guide

Last updated: 2026-04-01

---

## Purpose

Defines the register for all client-facing written output. Deliverables must be clear, professional, and written for the client — not for the consultant.

---

## Audience

Client decision-makers and their teams. They are experts in their domain but not in ours. They want to know what to do and why, not how we figured it out.

---

## Voice

**What it sounds like:** Direct. Confident in recommendations. Structured so the reader can navigate without reading every word. Professional without being stiff.

**What it does not sound like:** Academic. Hedged unnecessarily. Full of methodology description the client didn't ask for.

---

## Conventions

- Lead with the recommendation or finding, then support it — never build to a conclusion
- Use the client's own terminology when they have established terms; introduce new terms only when necessary and define them immediately
- Write in active voice: "We recommend" not "It is recommended"
- Sentences under 25 words wherever possible — clients skim; make skimming safe

---

## What to avoid

- "In order to..." — use "To..."
- "It should be noted that..." — just note it
- Percentage precision beyond what the underlying data warrants (don't write "37.4%" if "roughly 40%" is more honest)
- Jargon from our methodology that the client didn't ask about and doesn't need
